Foods of the Town

Cao lầu is a signature dish of the town, consisting of rice noodles, meat, greens, bean sprouts, and herbs, often served with a little broth made from pork and bone broth,with a strong resemblance to Japanese udon. The water for the broth has been traditionally taken from the Ba Le Well, thought to have been built in the 10th century by the Chams. Local Chinese residents do not claim it as a Chinese dish. Some Japanese researchers note similarities with noodles from the Ise region, but the flavor and preparation methods differ significantly.
